Hick's Law

Hick's Law sometimes called the Hick-Hyman Law states that the more stimuli to choose from, the longer it takes the user to make a decision on which one to interact with. The formula is RT = a + b log2(n). RT is reaction time, (n) is the number of options, a and b are other measurable factors in the situation. For Design, Hick's Law simply means optimizing a page to have less choices for the user to make so their reaction is faster and they can get the information that they need quicker.

The Rule of Thirds

The Rule of Thirds is a method that designers and photographers use to make aesthetic designs. To use the rule of thirds divide up a site into thirds vertically and horizontally and place dots where the lines cross. These are where the main focal points of the site or image should be placed. The human eye is automatically drawn to these points, however it sees the top left corner to be 41% more appealing, the top right 20%, bottom left 25%, and the bottom right 14%.
