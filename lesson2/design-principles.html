Hick's Law

Quizlet

quizlet.com

Hick's Law sometimes called the Hick-Hyman Law states that the more stimuli to choose from, the longer it takes the user to make a decision on which one to interact with. Quizlet's log-in interface only has three options to link an account with and one manual sign in to keep the page simple.

Fitts' Law

D&D Beyond

dndbeyond.com

Fitts’s Law says that the time it takes a user to move their mouse to a button is the distance to the button divided by the size of the button. The larger the distance and the smaller the size of the button, the longer the reaction time. D&D Beyond has the most important buttons for rolling ability checks taking up most of the screen. They are also right beside eachother to minimise clicking distance.

The Rule of Thirds

Wolfram Alpha

wolframalpha.com

The Rule of Thirds is a method that designers and photographers use to make aesthetic designs. To use the rule of thirds divide up a site into thirds vertically and horizontally and place dots where the lines cross. These are where the main focal points of the site or image should be placed. The human eye is automatically drawn to these points. On Wolfram Alpha's page the grids cross on the most useful buttons: The Step-by-Step Solutions, Units & Measurements, and More Topics.
